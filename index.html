About the Indian Army

The Indian Army is the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces. With over 1.4 million active personnel, it is the world's second-largest standing army and has the largest volunteer army. The Indian Army's primary mission is to ensure national security and national unity, defend the nation from external aggression and internal threats, and maintain peace and security within its borders.

The Army has been involved in four wars with neighboring Pakistan, one with China, and numerous peacekeeping missions worldwide. It also plays a crucial role in rescue operations during natural calamities and other disturbances.

Missions & Operations

Operation Vijay

The Kargil War victory in 1999 showcased India's military strength and tactical excellence in high-altitude warfare against Pakistani intruders.

Operation Meghdoot

Launched in 1984 to secure control of the Siachen Glacier, the highest battleground on Earth at 20,000 feet above sea level.

Surgical Strikes

Precision military strikes conducted in 2016 and 2019 to neutralize terror launchpads across the border, demonstrating India's strategic capability.

UN Peacekeeping

India is one of the largest contributors to UN peacekeeping missions, with over 200,000 troops serving in 49 missions across 4 continents.

Defense Systems

S-400 Triumf

Advanced long-range air defense missile system from Russia capable of tracking and destroying aerial threats up to 400 km away at altitudes of up to 30 km.

BrahMos

World's fastest supersonic cruise missile (Mach 2.8-3.0) jointly developed by India and Russia. Capable of being launched from land, sea, and air platforms.

Akash

Indigenous medium-range surface-to-air missile system with a range of 30-35 km, capable of engaging multiple aerial targets simultaneously.

Agni Series

Family of medium to intercontinental range ballistic missiles (Agni-I to Agni-V) with ranges from 700 km to 5,000+ km, ensuring strategic deterrence.

Maha Vir Chakra - Second highest gallantry award for acts of conspicuous gallantry in presence of enemy
Vir Chakra - Third in precedence for acts of gallantry in battlefield under enemy fire
Shaurya Chakra - Peacetime equivalent of Vir Chakra for gallantry away from battlefield
